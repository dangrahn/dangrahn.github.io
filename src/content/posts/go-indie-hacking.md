## At 42, I'm Finally Done Playing It Safe

I handed in my notice last week. After 20 years of building other people's products, I'm going all-in on indie hacking.

This probably sounds reckless. I'm 42. I have a wife and three kids. I've spent two decades climbing a ladder that pays well and comes with all the "security" everyone told me to chase.

But here's what nobody tells you about security: it can slowly kill your motivation while you're too comfortable to notice.

---

## The Problem Nobody Wants to Admit

I've worked everywhere. Digital agencies shipping client projects. Large product organisations with endless meetings. VC-backed startups burning through runway. I even spent four years co-founding a sweat equity startup studio.

The pattern was always the same: start excited, slowly lose steam, wonder why Sunday nights feel heavy.

So I went freelance. Consultancy work. Better money. More "freedom."

Same problem.

The lack of motivation followed me. It wasn't the company. It wasn't the clients. It wasn't even the work itself.

It was that none of it was truly *mine*.

I was building wealth and products for everyone except myself. And somewhere along the way, I stopped caring about the craft I used to love.

---

## The VC Dream That Never Fit

Here's something I've never really said out loud: I've had startup ambitions for years. The kind where you raise money, hire fast, and chase exponential growth.

But I never raised for any of my own ideas. Not once.

I always told myself I was being strategic. Waiting for the right moment. The right idea.

The truth? I'm too risk-averse to spend other people's money before I have something tangible to sell. The pressure of investor expectations, the need to grow at all costs, the loss of control—none of it sat right with me.

I've watched enough VC-backed startups implode to know that path isn't for everyone. It certainly isn't for me.

---

## What I Actually Want

I want to build products people pay for.

I want to wake up excited about my work again.

I want to be around other builders who understand why we do this—not for the exit, but for the craft and the freedom.

And yes, I want to make money. Real profits. Not vanity metrics or "we're growing users." Actual revenue that supports my family and gives me control over my time.

That's indie hacking to me: profitable products, creative freedom, and working alongside people who get it.

---

## The Reality of Making This Switch

Let me be clear: this isn't some overnight epiphany.

I've been trying to make this work on the side for years. Building after the kids go to bed. Squeezing in an hour before work. Sacrificing weekends.

It doesn't work. Not really.

When you're mentally drained from a full day of work, you don't have the creative energy to build something meaningful. When you're constantly context-switching between your job, your side project, and your family, everything suffers.

I was stuck in a loop: too tired to build, too scared to quit, too frustrated to stay.

Something had to give.

---

## Why Community Changes Everything

One thing I figured out early: this journey is brutal alone.

The indie hackers who make it aren't just good builders. They share publicly. They learn from each other. They celebrate small wins and talk openly about failures.

I'm committing to that. Building in public. Sharing what works and what doesn't. Being part of a community that understands why a stranger on the internet getting their first paying customer can make your whole week.

This isn't just strategy. It's survival. The accountability and connection keep you going when motivation disappears.

---

## What's Next

I don't have all the answers. I don't have a guaranteed plan that ensures this works.

What I have is:
- 20 years of building experience
- A family that supports this bet
- Enough runway to give this a real shot
- A community of indie hackers I genuinely want to grow alongside

I'm not chasing unicorns anymore. I'm chasing freedom. The freedom to build what I want, when I want, with people I respect.

If that resonates with you, follow along. I'll be sharing the wins, the losses, and everything in between.

Let's build something.
